# Help Us Keep Our Farm Alive!

**A Quick Guide for Our Wonderful Neighbor**

Thank you so much for looking after the garden while we're away. Everything here is designed to be low-maintenance, but the plants do need a daily check-in during summer. The whole routine takes about **10-15 minutes** -- and you're welcome to eat anything you pick!

---

## Daily Routine Checklist

Run through this list once a day, ideally in the morning or evening (not midday sun):

1. **Water the two BIG metal beds** near the trampoline (the rusty-brown steel boxes in the backyard). These two have a built-in water tank. Check the indicator tube on the side -- if the water level is low, fill through the pipe sticking up on the side. Only water the soil surface if it looks dry on top.

2. **Check the third BIG metal bed** near the trampoline (the one with strawberries and raspberries). This one does NOT have a water tank. Poke your finger 2cm into the soil -- if it's dry, give it a good water. In hot weather, this is usually every day.

3. **Water the two SMALL terrace beds** (the smaller galvanized steel boxes on the roof terrace, up the stairs above the carport). These dry out fast because they're small and exposed to wind. **Water every day in hot weather** (25C+). Check the soil -- if the top 2cm feels dry, water thoroughly.

4. **Pick ripe cherry tomatoes (tomat)** from the big center bed near the trampoline. Ripe = orange to deep red, slightly soft when gently squeezed. If it comes off easily with a gentle twist, it's ready.

5. **Pick ripe cucumbers (agurk)** from the same center bed -- they climb up the wooden A-frame trellis. Ripe = 10-12cm long, firm, dark green. Pick them before they get fat and yellow.

6. **Pick ripe strawberries (jordbaer) and raspberries (hindbaer)** from the third big bed. Ripe = red all over, comes off with a gentle tug. Don't pull hard -- if it resists, it's not ready yet.

7. **Quick scan** -- walk past all 5 beds and look for anything wilting badly. If a plant looks droopy and sad, give it extra water. That usually fixes it.

**Timing:** The whole thing should take about 10-15 minutes. Thank you so much!

### Bed B -- "The Raspberry Bed"

**Where it is:** The big rusty-brown metal box on the **west side** (leftmost) of the three beds near the trampoline. 120cm long, 60cm wide, 40cm tall. Has tall raspberry canes growing up from it.

[PHOTO: Take a photo of Bed B showing the raspberry canes and the chive border around the edges]

**What's growing:**
- 3 raspberry canes (hindbaer) -- Autumn Bliss variety, tall upright stems with berries
- Chives (purloeg) around the edges -- small clumps with purple flower balls

**Watering:** No water tank. Check soil 2cm down -- water if dry. Raspberries are fairly drought-tolerant, so this bed needs less water than the others. Every 2-3 days in normal weather, daily only in extreme heat (30C+).

**Harvesting:**
- **Raspberries:** Pick when deep red and they come off with a very gentle tug. Check every 1-2 days -- they ripen quickly and go soft fast.
- **Chive flowers:** You can snip them if you like -- they're edible and taste mildly oniony.
- Take berries home, eat them on the spot, or leave them on our counter!

**Leave alone:** Don't cut or bend the raspberry canes. Don't pull up the chives. If a cane leans over, gently prop it back upright.

### Bed C -- "The Tomato & Cucumber Bed"

**Where it is:** The big rusty-brown metal box in the **center** of the three beds near the trampoline. 120cm long, 60cm wide, 40cm tall. Has a wooden A-frame trellis over one end with cucumbers climbing it, and a tall staked tomato plant.

[PHOTO: Take a photo of Bed C showing the A-frame trellis with cucumbers, the staked tomato plant, and the pepper plant]

**What's growing:**
- 2 cucumber plants (agurk) climbing the A-frame trellis
- 1 cherry tomato plant (tomat) -- tall, staked, with clusters of small orange/red fruit
- 1 sweet pepper plant (peberfrugt) -- shorter, bushy, with green/red peppers
- 2 basil plants (basilikum) near the tomato
- Borage (hjulkrone) -- blue star-shaped flowers, good for bees
- Dill (dild) -- feathery herb

**Watering:** This bed has a built-in water tank (reservoir). Check the indicator tube on the side of the bed -- it shows the water level. If the level is low, pour water into the fill pipe sticking up on the side until water comes out the overflow. Only water the soil surface if it looks very dry on top. The tank does most of the work.

**Harvesting:**
- **Cherry tomatoes:** Pick when orange to deep red. They should come off easily with a gentle twist. Check daily -- they ripen fast in summer heat.
- **Cucumbers:** Pick when 10-12cm long, firm, and dark green. They hang down from the trellis -- easy to spot. Don't let them get big and yellow (that means they're overripe).
- **Peppers:** Pick when they've turned from green to red (or when they're large and glossy green if you prefer them mild). One gentle twist to snap off.
- **Basil/herbs:** Leave them unless you want some for cooking -- just pinch off leaves, don't pull the plant.

**Leave alone:** Don't move or adjust the trellis. Don't cut the tomato plant (even the side branches). Don't pull out the blue-flowered plant (borage) -- it's supposed to be there.

### Bed D -- "The Herb & Salad Terrace Bed"

**Where it is:** The **smaller galvanized steel box** (silver/grey) at the **south end** of the roof terrace (up the stairs above the carport). 80cm long, 40cm wide, 30cm tall. Positioned along the railing.

[PHOTO: Take a photo of Bed D on the terrace showing the herbs, spring onions, and leek plants]

**What's growing:**
- Spring onions (foraarloeg) -- thin green shoots in a row
- Leeks (porrer) -- thicker upright green stalks
- Creeping thyme (timian) at the corners
- Viola flowers at the edges

**Watering:** No water tank. These small terrace beds dry out quickly in wind and sun. **Water every day in hot weather (25C+).** Check the top 2cm of soil -- if dry, water thoroughly until water drains from the bottom. In cooler weather, every other day is fine.

**Harvesting:**
- **Spring onions:** You can pull one or two if you like -- they're tasty in salads.
- **Everything else:** Leave it. The leeks won't be ready until autumn.

**Leave alone:** Don't pull the leeks (the thick stalks) -- they need months to grow. Don't trim the thyme or flowers.

### Bed E -- "The Bean & Broccoli Terrace Bed"

**Where it is:** The **other small galvanized steel box** (silver/grey) at the **north end** of the roof terrace, about 80cm away from Bed D along the same railing. 80cm long, 40cm wide, 30cm tall.

[PHOTO: Take a photo of Bed E on the terrace showing the bean row, broccoli plant, and companion flowers]

**What's growing:**
- Bush beans (boenner) -- a row of bushy plants along one edge, with small beans hanging down
- 1 broccoli plant (broccoli) -- single plant with a thick stem and green head
- Borage (hjulkrone) -- blue star-shaped flowers
- Calendula -- orange/yellow daisy-like flowers
- Dill (dild) -- feathery herb

**Watering:** No water tank. Same as Bed D -- **water every day in hot weather (25C+)**. Check the top 2cm of soil. These small terrace beds dry out fast.

**Harvesting:**
- **Beans:** Pick when pods are 10-12cm long, firm, and snap cleanly when bent. Check every 2-3 days -- picking encourages more beans! The more you pick, the more they grow.
- **Broccoli:** If the main head is tight and green, it can wait. If it starts to open and show yellow flowers, cut the whole head off with a knife about 10cm below. Side shoots will keep growing after that. Text us a photo if you're unsure.
- **Everything else:** Leave the flowers and herbs.

**Leave alone:** Don't pull out any plants. The flowers (borage, calendula) are companions -- they help the vegetables.

## Emergency & Contact

**If something looks seriously wrong:**
Text [PHONE NUMBER] with a photo. Don't worry -- most things can wait until we're back. Plants are tougher than they look.

**If we've had no rain for a week AND it's very hot (30C+):**
Give every bed a deep soak -- water slowly until the soil is thoroughly wet and water starts draining out. For the two reservoir beds (the center and leftmost big beds near the trampoline), fill the reservoir pipes completely.

**If a plant falls over:**
Just prop it back up against the trellis, stake, or railing. A bit of string or a clip works if you have one handy.

**If you see pests:**
Ignore them unless something is being completely devoured. A few holes in leaves are normal. If a plant is covered in bugs, give it a spray with the garden hose. That's usually enough.
