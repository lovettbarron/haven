### Nature Corner

The nature corner (log pile, stone pile, insect hotel) is located in the **northeast corner** of the backyard, behind the swing set along the far-right boundary fence. This shaded, sheltered area is ideal for wildlife habitat.

### Roof Terrace

The roof terrace above the carport is available for seating and furniture. No raised beds are placed on the terrace.

## Sun Exposure Zones

| Zone | Location | Summer Sun | Notes |
|------|----------|------------|-------|
| **Full sun** | Grass area near house/terrace (Bed B position) | 6-8+ hours direct | Open position with unobstructed sun exposure all day; strongest growing position |
| **Morning sun + afternoon shade** | Grass area near cobblestones (Bed A position) | 5-7 hours direct | Good morning sun from east and midday sun from south; shade developing around 14:00 from house |
| **Shade** | Under mature tree canopy along east and north boundaries | 2-4 hours direct | Not suitable for food crops; good for nature corner |

**Key observations:**
- The house faces **west** toward the street. The backyard extends **east and northeast**.
- Morning sun comes from the east. Afternoon sun from the south and west is strongest.
- **Bed B** (near the house) sits in the most open, full-sun position -- ideal for strawberries, sunflowers, and sensory plants that thrive in maximum light.
- **Bed A** (near the cobblestones, farther from house) gets good morning and midday sun, but the **house casts afternoon shade from the south around 14:00**. This is beneficial: the middle zone of Bed A holds lettuce, broccoli, and leeks, which prefer relief from intense afternoon sun.
- The warm crops in Bed A's **north zone** (fence side) get the best sun exposure -- positioned for maximum direct sun for tomatoes, cucumbers, and peppers.
- The **south zone** of Bed A (closest to house) gets the most afternoon shade -- ideal for shade-tolerant raspberries.
