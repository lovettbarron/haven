Create `docs/daily-routine-card.md` -- a printable single-page daily routine card for the child's 5-minute morning garden check.

Structure:
1. Title: "My Garden Morning / Min Have-Morgen" with a note that this is a 5-minute routine
2. Five steps, each with a large emoji icon, a one-word English action, and a short Danish verbal cue for the father:
   - Step 1: LOOK (eyes emoji) -- Walk past all beds. What changed overnight? (Father cue in Danish)
   - Step 2: TOUCH (hand emoji) -- Feel the soil in each bed. Dry = water time! (Father cue in Danish)
   - Step 3: WATER (water droplet emoji) -- Small watering can, gentle pour on dry beds (Father cue in Danish)
   - Step 4: PICK (basket emoji) -- Any ripe strawberries? Radish tops poking out? Lettuce to snip? (Father cue in Danish)
   - Step 5: MEASURE (ruler emoji) -- How tall is the sunflower today? Mark on the ruler stick (Father cue in Danish)
3. A "This Week's Special" box -- left blank for the father to fill in verbally or with a sticky note. Include 3 seasonal examples: "W18: Can you find sunflower sprouts?", "W24: Count the green tomatoes", "W34: Which strawberry plant has the most berries?"
4. Father's notes section at the bottom:
   - Reminder: Any step can be skipped. The walk is the routine, not completing every action.
   - Reminder: If he finishes in 2 minutes, he is done. If he wants to stay longer, that is his choice.
   - Reminder: Never extend past 5 minutes even if there is more to do.
   - Lamination tip: Print on A4, laminate if possible for outdoor use.

Use the existing crop JSON `child_actions` prompts for the Danish verbal cues. Reference beds as A-E throughout.

Design constraints from research:
- Visual-first: emoji icons are the primary communication, not text
- Predictable: same 5 steps every single day, never varies
- Agency: the child decides what to pick, what to measure, what to look at
- No checkboxes for the child (this is a walk-through, not a form)
- Fits on one A4 page when printed

Create `docs/weekly-garden-walk.md` -- a printable weekly garden walk checklist with 3-5 recurring activities, each taking 2-3 minutes for a total of 5-10 minutes.

IMPORTANT: Bed-to-crop assignments verified against data/beds/*.json. Use these exact mappings:
- Bed A ("His Bed"): strawberry, lamb's ear, lemon balm, thyme, viola, calendula, pea shoots, sunflower, nasturtium, mint (sunken pot at edge)
- Bed B ("Raspberry Bed"): raspberry, chives
- Bed C ("Shared Bed"): peas, borage, kale, cucumber, tomato, basil, pepper
- Bed D ("Terrace Herb Bed"): lettuce, spring onion, thyme, viola, leek, radish
- Bed E ("Terrace Flower Bed"): borage, calendula, broccoli, bush beans, dill

Structure:
1. Title: "Weekend Garden Walk / Weekend Have-Tur" with a note that this is a 10-minute maximum activity
2. Four recurring activities, each with emoji icon, name, what to do, and which beds:
   - SENSORY WALK (nose emoji): Touch lamb's ear in Bed A, smell mint pot near Bed A, crush lemon balm leaf in Bed A, smell thyme in Bed A or Bed D. Father says the Danish plant names. (2-3 min)
   - CREATURE CHECK (butterfly emoji): Look for bees on borage (Bed C or Bed E), ladybugs on nasturtium (Bed A), butterflies on calendula (Bed A or Bed E). Check the bug hotel and water station. (2-3 min)
   - HARVEST ROUND (basket emoji): Walk all 5 beds and pick anything ripe. Strawberries (Bed A), radishes (Bed A/D), lettuce (Bed D), peas (Bed C), herbs for dinner (Bed D). Season-dependent -- father guides which beds have harvestable crops. (2-3 min)
   - GROWTH CHECK (ruler emoji): Measure sunflower height (Bed A). Compare tallest tomato plant to the child's height (Bed C). Count fruits on pepper plants (Bed C). Note what is flowering vs fruiting. (2-3 min)
3. Seasonal highlights section with 3-4 season blocks:
   - Spring (W16-W20): Look for first sprouts, count germinated seeds, find the first flower
   - Early summer (W21-W28): Watch fruits form, first harvests, bee counting on borage
   - Peak summer (W28-W36): Harvest party, tallest sunflower measurement, taste test different tomatoes
   - Autumn (W37-W44): Last harvests, seed saving, goodbye to annual plants
4. Father's notes:
   - Pick 2-3 activities per walk, not all 4. Let the child choose which ones.
   - If he loses interest after one activity, the walk is done.
   - The walk happens once per week, ideally Saturday or Sunday morning.

Use crop JSON `engagement.sensory` fields and `child_actions` for accurate activity descriptions. Reference beds by canonical A-E names with correct crop assignments per bed data files.

Design constraints:
- Visual-first with emoji markers
- Each activity is self-contained (can do any 2-3, skip the rest)
- Time-boxed: 10 minutes maximum, each activity 2-3 minutes
- Fits on one A4 page when printed (seasonal highlights can be a second page)
