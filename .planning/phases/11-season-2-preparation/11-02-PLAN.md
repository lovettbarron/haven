  <name>Task 2: Add crop failure recovery section to troubleshooting guide</name>
  <files>docs/troubleshooting-guide.md</files>
  <action>
Read the full docs/troubleshooting-guide.md first to understand the existing format, severity system, and structure.

Add a new major section "## Crop Failure Recovery" at the END of the troubleshooting guide (after all existing sections). This section must match the existing format (severity tags, "What to do" numbered lists, "Child action" prompts, photo placeholders).

**Opening framing (required per CONTEXT.md):**
> "It's OK -- this happens to every garden." Plants fail sometimes. It does not mean you did anything wrong. An empty spot in the bed is an opportunity, not a problem. You can replant, or you can leave it -- both are fine.

Emphasize that replacement is OPTIONAL. Empty space is acceptable in a learning garden.

**Month-by-month replanting windows** (May through August) as a reference table:
| Month | Window | Good Replacements | Days to Harvest |
- May: After last frost (~May 15). Radish, baby lettuce, arugula, basil seedling. 20-30 days.
- June: Full season. Radish, baby lettuce, bush beans, quick basil, cilantro. 20-45 days.
- July: Mid-season. Radish, baby lettuce, arugula, quick spinach, cilantro, microgreens (indoor). 20-40 days.
- August: Last outdoor window. Radish, baby lettuce, arugula, spinach, kale (autumn harvest). 25-50 days.

**Quick-grower suggestions not in original plan:**
- Microgreens (indoor, any time, 7-14 days) -- excellent crisis recovery "We can grow something right now!"
- Arugula/rocket/rucola -- 21 days to baby leaf
- Quick radish (Cherry Belle, French Breakfast) -- 21-25 days
- Cilantro/koriander -- 21-30 days, direct sow May-July

**Common failure scenarios** (each as a subsection matching existing troubleshooting format):
1. "A seedling died" -- damping off, slug damage, frost damage. Severity: :green_circle: No rush. Emotional framing first, then check the month table for replacements.
2. "Something is eating my plant" -- caterpillars on broccoli, aphid overwhelming, slug devastation. Severity: :yellow_circle: This week. Cross-reference existing pest sections, then offer replacement if plant is beyond saving.
3. "The plant looks sick" -- powdery mildew, blight, botrytis. Severity: :yellow_circle: This week. Remove affected parts, assess if plant is recoverable, offer replacement if not.
4. "The plant bolted / looks weird" -- heat bolt on lettuce, cold damage on warm crops. Severity: :green_circle: No rush. Explain what bolting is, suggest replacement timing.

Each scenario gets: severity, "It's OK" framing, what happened, what to do (numbered), replacement options (with month table reference), child action prompt.

Include Danish names where relevant (rucola, koriander, radise).
