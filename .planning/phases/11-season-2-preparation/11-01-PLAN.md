  <action>
Create docs/soil-refresh-guide.md documenting the full test-and-amend protocol per CONTEXT.md locked decisions.

Structure as a reference document (similar to soil-layers.md and reservoir-build.md pattern):

**Two-step timing:**
1. **Fall amendment (W44-W46, after cleanup):** Rake bed surface smooth after plant removal. Add 2-5cm compost over entire bed surface. Cover with 5-10cm straw mulch. Compost feeds soil organisms over winter.
2. **Spring amendment (W14-W15, before planting):** Remove/rake winter mulch. Check soil level -- document that 5-10cm drop is NORMAL (garden waste layer decomposition from Year 1, per soil-layers.md). Top-dress with 5-10cm fresh hojbedsmuld. Optional soil test. Heavy feeder zones get additional compost or slow-release fertilizer.

**Bed-specific guidance:**
- **Bed A north zone (reservoir):** Stays wetter from sub-irrigation. May compact more. Check reservoir pipes intact after winter. Top-dressing sits on existing geotextile/gravel system.
- **Bed A middle/south zones:** Standard top-dress. Raspberry zone (south) gets extra compost around canes after February pruning.
- **Bed B:** 360 liters total, smaller volume depletes faster. May need up to 10cm top-dressing. Monitor quality more closely.

**Danish products and suppliers:**
- Hojbedsmuld (raised bed soil mix) -- Plantorama, Bauhaus garden section
- Kompost/havekompost -- garden centers or homemade
- Slow-release fertilizer (havegodning/langtidsgodning) for heavy feeder zones
- Soil test kits: simple pH kits at garden centers (30-50 DKK), full analysis via Eurofins Miljoe postal service

**Optional soil testing section:** pH test instructions, nutrient interpretation guide, amendment recommendations based on results.

Cross-reference docs/soil-layers.md as the original recipe baseline. Use Bed A/B canonical naming.
